Part 4: Wealth as time

Another way of thinking of the example above, is that a single billionaire could afford to live comfortably in America, with no additional income, for over 11,000 years (not adjusting for inflation)!

In our society, that old adage, “money is time,” really is true. Most non-billionaires exchange their time, in the form of hours spent working, for money. That means, in a real sense, money represents the accumulation of someone’s time and effort.

The Federal minimum wage in America is $7.25 an hour. That’s $58 a day. About $14,500 a year.

That means a billionaire could hire 17,241,379 workers for a single day of work

That’s more than the combined populations of the three most populous cities in the U.S.

It would take almost 68,966 years of full time work at minimum wage to earn $1 billion. That’s about 1,000 lifetimes of work.

Part 5: Conclusion

One billion dollars is a lot! It is magnitudes more than what we consider “rich” in this country. It’s enough to fund a small city for a year, or hire an army of workers. It represents an enormous amount of time and work by an enormous amount of people. When one person controls all that wealth, they have almost unlimited potential to shape the world around them, because their gathering and spending of that wealth in turn determines how other people spend their time. All in all, it’s fairly un-democratic, because in this way, a lot of money means a lot of power.
